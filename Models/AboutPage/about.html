About this masterclass

Target Audience

This masterclass is designed for anyone who wants to build a meaningful understanding of automotive cybersecurity. While some modules — particularly at higher DOK levels — touch on concepts that benefit from prior technical or regulatory exposure, no prior expertise is required to get started.

Where deeper knowledge becomes relevant, we provide the tools, definitions, and references needed to engage with the material and explore further where necessary. Content is structured using Webb's Depth of Knowledge (DOK) framework, which classifies material by the cognitive complexity of the content rather than the background of the individual reader.

Webb’s Depth of Knowledge

Webb’s Depth of Knowledge, also referred to as DOK levels, has allowed us to organise the masterclass in a way that reflects how deep understanding is developed — starting with the broader landscape and progressing towards professional-level analysis. Each passage is labelled with its corresponding DOK level(s), and the label serves as a guide for how to engage with the content rather than a measure of how difficult it may feel.

At DOK 1, you are building your vocabulary. Passages at this level cover the connected vehicle landscape, core terminology, and the regulatory environment. These passages are intended to help you recognise and remember key concepts. — no analysis required yet.

At DOK 2, you are starting to work with the material. Passages here ask you to compare attack surfaces, classify threats, and understand how frameworks like ISO/SAE 21434 are structured. You are expected to draw connections between concepts.

At DOK 3, you are reasoning under uncertainty. These passages introduce threat modelling, risk assessment, and attack path analysis — areas where there is no single correct answer and where you must justify your thinking from evidence.

At DOK 4, you are producing original analysis. This is where the masterclass connects directly to professional practice: conducting a TARA, evaluating a real system's security posture, and contributing to a cybersecurity case.

Acknowledgements

Parts of the content presented in this masterclass build on the work of a previous project group, whose original materials can be found at masterclass.starryhill.nl. Their work serves as the foundation for this iteration, and all content drawn from it is properly referenced throughout this website to ensure transparency and authenticity.

Rather than duplicating what has already been done, this project takes their contribution as a starting point and builds further. We have reviewed the existing masterclass critically, identified gaps in scope, depth, and currency, and expanded the material to address those areas. The aim is to preserve the strengths of the original while extending it with updated insights, new perspectives, and additional resources that reflect the current state of the field.
