About this masterclass

Target Audience

This masterclass was built by students, for anyone who wants to build a meaningful understanding of automotive cybersecurity — from the foundations of connected vehicle architecture to the regulatory frameworks, threat landscapes, and open questions shaping the field today. No prior technical or regulatory expertise is required. Some modules at higher DOK levels assume familiarity with earlier content, but everything you need is here.

Where deeper knowledge becomes relevant, we provide the tools, definitions, and references needed to engage with the material and explore further where necessary. Content is structured using Webb's Depth of Knowledge (DOK) framework, which classifies material by the cognitive complexity of the content rather than the background of the individual reader.

Webb’s Depth of Knowledge

Webb’s Depth of Knowledge, also referred to as DOK levels, has allowed us to organise the masterclass in a way that reflects how deep understanding is developed — starting with the broader landscape and progressing towards professional-level analysis. Each passage is labelled with its corresponding DOK level(s), and the label serves as a guide for how to engage with the content rather than a measure of how difficult it may feel.

At DOK 1, you are building your vocabulary. Passages at this level introduce core concepts and terminology. They are intended to help you recognise and recall key ideas — no analysis required yet.

At DOK 2, you are working with the material. Passages here ask you to understand how systems are structured, how components relate to each other, and what those relationships mean in practice. You are expected to apply concepts correctly and draw connections between them.

At DOK 3, you are reasoning under uncertainty. These passages introduce threat modelling, risk assessment, and attack path analysis — areas where expertise informs your thinking but does not determine the answer. You are expected to evaluate trade-offs, justify conclusions from incomplete information, and recognise where reasonable positions differ.

At DOK 4, you are producing original analysis. Depending on the module, this means evaluating a real system's security posture and reasoning through architectural trade-offs under real constraints, or engaging with the political, ethical, and geopolitical dimensions of connected vehicle governance — questions that sit outside engineering frameworks and require you to form and defend a position.

Acknowledgements

Parts of the content presented in this masterclass build on the work of a previous project group. Their work serves as the foundation for this iteration, and all content drawn from it is properly referenced throughout this website to ensure transparency and authenticity.

Rather than duplicating what has already been done, this project takes their contribution as a starting point and builds further. We have reviewed the existing masterclass critically, identified gaps in scope, depth, and currency, and expanded the material to address those areas. The aim is to preserve the strengths of the original while extending it with updated insights, new perspectives, and additional resources that reflect the current state of the field.
